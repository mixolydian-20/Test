Purpose

This site was created to help HR professionals use AI tools safely and effectively, consistent with University policy, data classifications, and our values of equity and transparency. HR professionals throughout the University of Minnesota uses AI to improve service for and with our fellow employees, reduce risk, and learn together.

This HR site mirrors the structure of Navigating AI @ UMN and adds HR‑specific guardrails, templates, and role‑based examples.

Scope

Applies to HR work across Talent Acquisition, Classification & Compensation, Benefits, Employee & Labor Relations, HR Operations, HR Reporting & Analytics, Payroll & Time and Absence Management, Performance Management, and Learning & Development.

Owners: Office of Human Resources (OHR) with partners in OIT and EOT.

Start here

  • Data first. Only Public data may be entered into AI by default. Never paste Private‑Restricted or Private‑Highly Restricted data into unapproved tools.
  • Prefer UMN‑licensed tools with enterprise protections (see “Approved tools”).
  • Keep a human in the loop. AI never makes the final employment decision.
  • Don’t integrate unapproved tools with University systems; coordinate with OIT for any pilots.

Questions about data? See the Data Security Classification resource.

What you Must / May / Must‑Not do

MUST

  • Classify data before sharing with AI; strip identifiers where feasible.
  • Disclose and document meaningful AI assistance in hiring or other decision files.
  • Keep human oversight for hiring, promotion, discipline, pay, and leave decisions.
  • Use UMN‑licensed tools with Enterprise Data Protection where available.

MAY

  • Draft job ads/JDs, summarize policies, brainstorm training ideas with human review.
  • Summarize public dashboards or anonymized samples (never upload restricted data to unapproved tools).
  • Generate plain‑language guides; link to official sources and verify accuracy.

MUST‑NOT

  • Use AI to screen or rank applicants/resumes or automate employment decisions.
  • Paste Private‑Restricted/Highly Restricted data into unapproved tools.
  • Use AI outputs without human fact‑checking and attribution where appropriate.

HR use cases by domain

Risk levels (Low/Medium/High) guide caution and required approvals. Human review is always required.

Talent Acquisition (TA)

  • ● Medium   Draft job ads and job description language; remove biased terms; produce variants for different audiences. HR reviews before posting. Use TA‑001.
  • ● Low   Create interview practice questions and rubrics (no candidate PII).
  • ● High   Do not use AI to screen or rank applicants/resumes.

Classification & Compensation

  • ● Medium   Normalize JD text and summarize role families; comp analyst validates.
  • ● High   No AI‑generated pay recommendations.

Employee & Labor Relations (ELR)

  • ● Medium   Draft coaching memos or letters; ELR finalizes; avoid nonpublic details in unapproved tools.
  • ● High   Investigation summaries with confidential facts.

Benefits & Leaves

  • ● Low   Draft plain‑language summaries of public benefits info; link to official sources.
  • ● High   Eligibility determinations.

HR Operations / Records

  • ● Low   Draft knowledge‑base articles and how‑tos; records staff review prior to publishing.
  • ● High   Transcribe/summarize confidential meetings in unapproved tools.

Learning & Development (L&D)

  • ● Low   Create outlines, job aids, and microlearning copy; L&D reviews and cites sources.
  • ● Medium   Automated coaching text without manager verification.

HR Analytics & Workforce Data

  • ● Medium   Summarize public dashboards or de‑identified samples; verify against source.
  • ● High   Modeling/predictions on identifiable HR data outside secured, approved environments.

Payroll & Time

  • ● Low   Draft plain‑language guidance (time entry, FLSA explanations); HR validates.
  • ● High   Pay calculations or eligibility decisions generated by AI.

Governance, accountability & review

Required for Medium/High uses

  • Document a Human‑in‑the‑Loop review (use HIL‑003).
  • For High‑risk scenarios involving nonpublic data, obtain pre‑approval with OIT Security & OHR.

Incident & redress

  • Suspect bias, privacy risk, or misuse? Report Information Security Issues.
  • Discrimination/harassment concerns? Report to EO/Title IX.

Quarterly review & success metrics

  • % TA postings using TA‑001; # HIL‑003 sign‑offs; # vendor AI addenda executed.
  • # incidents and corrective actions; % HR staff trained on data classification & AI basics.
  • # knowledge‑base articles improved via AI with reviewer attribution.
